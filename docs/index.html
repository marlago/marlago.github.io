Sol Lago

Assistant Professor

Goethe University Frankfurt

I study sentence comprehension in native and non-native speakers of different languages. Together with my colleagues at the Romance Lab, I focus on the acquisition and processing of Romance languages like Italian, Spanish, Portuguese and French. For example, we investigate how bilingual children acquire German together with a Romance language, or how adult German speakers process Romance languages (and viceversa). To address these questions, we conduct comprehension and production experiments using behavioral, eye-tracking and neurophysiological methods.

  • New job: In April 2019 I started as an Assistant professor at Goethe University in Frankfurt.

  • Grant award: In 2017 I received a grant from the German Research Foundation to study how Spanish and English native speakers process gender and number agreement in German. I am using this research as a test case to address two important questions: Are some languages more difficult to understand and potentially learn than others? And can this difficulty lie in how a speaker's native language shapes their processing mechanisms? This work is being conducted at the University of Potsdam in collaboration with Kate Stone and Shravan Vasishth.
